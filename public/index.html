Biography

I am an energy and environmental economist broadly interested in how to effectively address climate change. My current work focuses on three streams: improving our understanding of how consumers respond to information and incentives in their energy use and conservation decisions, how the substitution between transport modes within international trade impacts trade flows and the regulation of carbon emissions from international transportation, and on how low investment carbon-footprint targets change the stocks chosen for sustainable investment funds.

Australian and Canadian missed climate targets

We hear a lot about plans to reduce greenhouse gas emissions, yet comparably little about how we’ve lived up to past commitments. It’s worth taking a look at our past (in)actions and goals as they’re important context in which to view current progress. It’s not an optimistic picture. Without a change in direction the Paris target is well on track to joining past failed targets, with voters and politicians again kicking the climate can down the road as a problem for future generations to pay the growing costs of.
